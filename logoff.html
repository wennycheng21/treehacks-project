Blockchain is a shared, immutable ledger for recording transactions and tracking assets. Virtually anything of value, from digital currency or cryptocurrency to media, can be tracked and traded on a blockchain network. The goal of the digitally distributed and decentralized system is to reduce risk and cut costs for those involved in transactions.

Features

Secure & Universal

Security

Once users make entries in the blockchain, the action can never be erased. The decentralized storage of blockchains across multiple computers ensures that no hacker can access the entire chain in one go.

Transparency

Our current financial system relies on third-party intermediaries who process transactions. Blockchain and cryptocurrencies offer complete user participation in the financial markets without intermediary involvement.

Bridging Assets

Blockchain can be implemented for virtually any digital asset, from cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin to media, fashion, gaming, and beyond.

The current crypto landscape is a walled garden.

Interoperability

Transmitting tokens between participants and executing smart contracts can only be carried out within a single blockchain because interoperability between several blockchains is not accounted for in existing protocols and standards.

Lack of integrated tracking system

Users have to manage multiple wallets, storing private keys and monitoring activity across diverse investments, without a streamlined tracking system.

User experience

Clunky user interfaces and jargon result in inaccessibility to cryptocurrency for those who aren’t digital natives.

The future is here

We are at the start of the next generation of the public internet: web3. In this third generation, data is stored and maintained by the fabric of the digital network itself. Users directly manage and control their data and information, which is then stored in a distributed blockchain distributed securely and equally across all participants, unable to be controlled by any central organization.

Definitions

Non-fungible tokens (NFTs) are blockchain-based tokens that each represent a unique asset like a piece of art, digital content, or media.

01. Exchange traded fund (ETF)

A basket of investments like stocks or bonds that lets you invest in lots of securities all at once.

02. Omnichain

The principal of a protocol offering the ease of use of transferring hustle-free assets between chains.

Omnichain dApps are digital applications that span all blockchains and allow for seamless transactions among them.
